Variational Calculus in VAE

Given a probability distribution p(x)p(x)p(x), we want to find an encoder qθ(z∣x)q_{\theta}(z|x)qθ​(z∣x) that approximates the true posterior p(z∣x)p(z|x)p(z∣x). And we resort to minimize the Kullback-Leibler divergence between them:

min⁡DKL(qθ(z∣x)∥p(z∣x))\min D_{KL}\left(q_{\theta}(z|x) \| p(z|x)\right) minDKL​(qθ​(z∣x)∥p(z∣x))

And DKL(qθ(z∣x)∥p(z∣x))D_{KL}\left(q_{\theta}(z|x) \| p(z|x)\right)DKL​(qθ​(z∣x)∥p(z∣x)) can be computed as:

DKL(qθ(z∣x)∥p(z∣x))=Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡qθ(z∣x)p(z∣x)]=∫qθ(z∣x)log⁡qθ(z∣x)p(z∣x)dz=J[q]s.t.∫qθ(z∣x)dz=1\begin{align*} D_{KL}\left(q_{\theta}(z|x) \| p(z|x)\right) &= \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\left[\log\frac{q_{\theta}(z|x)}{p(z|x)}\right]\\ &=\int q_{\theta}(z|x) \log\frac{q_{\theta}(z|x)}{p(z|x)} dz\\ &= J[q]\\ s.t. \int q_{\theta}(z|x) dz = 1 \end{align*} DKL​(qθ​(z∣x)∥p(z∣x))s.t.∫qθ​(z∣x)dz=1​=Eqθ​(z∣x)​[logp(z∣x)qθ​(z∣x)​]=∫qθ​(z∣x)logp(z∣x)qθ​(z∣x)​dz=J[q]​

We introduce a Lagrange multipler λ\lambdaλ, and we can write the constrained optimization problem as:

S[q,λ]=J[q]+λ(∫qθ(z∣x)dz−1)=∫[q(z)log⁡q(z)−q(z)log⁡p(z∣x)]dz+λ(∫q(z)dz−1)=∫[q(z)log⁡q(z)−q(z)log⁡p(z∣x)+λq(z)]dz−λ\begin{align*} S[q, \lambda] &= J[q] + \lambda\left(\int q_{\theta}(z|x) dz-1\right)\\ &= \int \left[ q(z) \log q(z) -q(z) \log p(z|x)\right] dz+ \lambda\left(\int q(z) dz- 1\right)\\ &= \int \left[ q(z) \log q(z) -q(z) \log p(z|x) + \lambda q(z) \right] dz - \lambda \end{align*} S[q,λ]​=J[q]+λ(∫qθ​(z∣x)dz−1)=∫[q(z)logq(z)−q(z)logp(z∣x)]dz+λ(∫q(z)dz−1)=∫[q(z)logq(z)−q(z)logp(z∣x)+λq(z)]dz−λ​

Use the Euler-Lagrange Equation w.r.t. qqq

∂L∂q−ddz∂L∂q˙=0\frac{\partial L}{\partial q} -\frac{d}{dz} \frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot q} = 0 ∂q∂L​−dzd​∂q˙​∂L​=0

Reduce to

∂L∂q=0\frac{\partial L}{\partial q} = 0 ∂q∂L​=0

since LLL is not explicitly dependent on q˙\dot qq˙​.

Therefore

log⁡q(z)+1−log⁡p(z∣x)+λ=0\log q(z) + 1 - \log p(z|x) + \lambda =0 logq(z)+1−logp(z∣x)+λ=0

We can solve the above equations and get

q(z)=exp⁡(log⁡p(z∣x)−1−λ)=C⋅p(z∣x)q(z) = \exp\left(\log p(z|x) - 1 - \lambda\right) = C \cdot p(z|x) q(z)=exp(logp(z∣x)−1−λ)=C⋅p(z∣x)

And with normalizing constraint,

∫q(z)dz=1\int q(z) dz = 1 ∫q(z)dz=1

we know that q(z)=p(z∣x)q(z) = p(z|x)q(z)=p(z∣x).

And the next step is to decompose

DKL(qθ(z∣x)∥p(z∣x))=Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡qθ(z∣x)p(z)]+Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡p(z)p(z∣x)]=DKL(qθ(z∣x)∥p(z))+Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡p(x)]=DKL(qθ(z∣x)∥p(z))+log⁡p(x)\begin{align*} D_{KL}\left(q_{\theta}(z|x) \| p(z|x)\right) &= \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta(z|x)}}\left[\log \frac{q_{\theta}(z|x)}{p(z)}\right] + \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta(z|x)}}\left[\log \frac{p(z)}{p(z|x)}\right] \\ &= D_{KL}\left(q_{\theta}(z|x) \| p(z)\right) + \mathbb{E}_{q_\theta(z|x)}\left[\log p(x)\right]\\ &= D_{KL}\left(q_{\theta}(z|x) \| p(z)\right) + \log p(x) \end{align*} DKL​(qθ​(z∣x)∥p(z∣x))​=Eqθ(z∣x)​​[logp(z)qθ​(z∣x)​]+Eqθ(z∣x)​​[logp(z∣x)p(z)​]=DKL​(qθ​(z∣x)∥p(z))+Eqθ​(z∣x)​[logp(x)]=DKL​(qθ​(z∣x)∥p(z))+logp(x)​

which is equivalent to maximizing the ELBO:

L(q)=−DKL(qθ(z∣x)∥p(z))=Ez∼q(z∣x)[log⁡p(x,z)qθ(z∣x)]\mathcal{L}(q) = - D_{KL}\left(q_{\theta}(z|x) \| p(z)\right) =\mathbb {E}_{z\sim q(z|x)}\left [ \log\frac{p(x,z)}{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\right] L(q)=−DKL​(qθ​(z∣x)∥p(z))=Ez∼q(z∣x)​[logqθ​(z∣x)p(x,z)​]

And ELBO can be expressed as:

L(q)=Ez∼q(z∣x)[log⁡pθ(x∣z)]−DKL(qθ(z∣x)∥p(z))\mathcal{L}(q) = \mathbb{E}_{z\sim q(z|x)} [\log p_{\theta}(x|z)] - D_{KL}(q_{\theta}(z|x) \| p(z)) L(q)=Ez∼q(z∣x)​[logpθ​(x∣z)]−DKL​(qθ​(z∣x)∥p(z))

And pθ(x∣z)p_{\theta}(x|z)pθ​(x∣z) represent the decoder.
