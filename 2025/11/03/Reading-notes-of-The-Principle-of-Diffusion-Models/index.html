2.1 Variational Autoencoder

Model structure

x→encoder,qθ(z∣x)z∼N(0,1)→decoder,pϕ(x∣z)x^x\xrightarrow{\mathrm{encoder},q_{\theta}(z|x)} z\sim \mathcal{N}(0,1) \xrightarrow{\mathrm{decoder}, p_{\phi}(x|z)} \hat{x} xencoder,qθ​(z∣x)​z∼N(0,1)decoder,pϕ​(x∣z)​x^

Key formulae:

The learned data distribution:

pϕ(x)=Ep(z)[pϕ(x∣z)]=∫pϕ(x∣z)p(z)dzp_{\phi}(x) = \mathbb{E}_{p(z)}[p_{\phi}(x|z)] = \int p_{\phi}(x|z)p(z)dz pϕ​(x)=Ep(z)​[pϕ​(x∣z)]=∫pϕ​(x∣z)p(z)dz

Intractable to compute due to integral over latent variable zzz.

Maximizing log-likelihood   ⟺  \iff⟺ minimizing the KL divergence between true data distribution q(x)q(x)q(x) and learned data distribution pϕ(x)p_{\phi}(x)pϕ​(x):

arg⁡max⁡ϕEq(x)[log⁡pϕ(x)]  ⟺  arg⁡min⁡ϕEq(x)[log⁡q(x)−log⁡pϕ(x)]  ⟺  arg⁡min⁡ϕKL(q(x)∣∣pϕ(x))\begin{aligned} \arg\max_{\phi} \mathbb{E}_{q(x)}[\log p_{\phi}(x)] &\iff \arg\min_{\phi} \mathbb{E}_{q(x)}[\log q(x) - \log p_{\phi}(x)] \\ &\iff \arg\min_{\phi} \mathrm{KL}(q(x) || p_{\phi}(x)) \end{aligned} argϕmax​Eq(x)​[logpϕ​(x)]​⟺argϕmin​Eq(x)​[logq(x)−logpϕ​(x)]⟺argϕmin​KL(q(x)∣∣pϕ​(x))​

Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO):

Core Idea:

We try to estimate pϕ(x)p_{\phi}(x)pϕ​(x) by Bayes’ theorem

pϕ(x)=pϕ(x,z)pϕ(z∣x)p_{\phi}(x) = \frac{p_{\phi}(x,z)}{p_{\phi}(z|x)} pϕ​(x)=pϕ​(z∣x)pϕ​(x,z)​

Now the posterior pϕ(z∣x)p_{\phi}(z|x)pϕ​(z∣x) is also intractable, so we introduce a variational distribution qθ(z∣x)q_{\theta}(z|x)qθ​(z∣x) to approximate it.

Proof of being lower bound of the log-likelihood log⁡pϕ(x)\log p_{\phi}(x)logpϕ​(x):

log⁡pϕ(x)=Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡pϕ(x,z)pϕ(z∣x)]=Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡pϕ(x,z)qθ(z∣x)⋅qθ(z∣x)pϕ(z∣x)]=Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡pϕ(x,z)qθ(z∣x)]+Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡qθ(z∣x)pϕ(z∣x)]=Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡pϕ(x,z)qθ(z∣x)]+KL(qθ(z∣x)∣∣pϕ(z∣x))≥Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡pϕ(x,z)qθ(z∣x)]=Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡pϕ(x∣z)]⏟Reconstruction Term−KL(qθ(z∣x)∣∣p(z))⏟Latent Regularization=L(θ,ϕ;x)\begin{aligned} \log p_{\phi}(x) &= \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\left[\log \frac{p_{\phi}(x,z)}{p_{\phi}(z|x)}\right] \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\left[\log \frac{p_{\phi}(x,z)}{q_{\theta}(z|x)} \cdot \frac{q_{\theta}(z|x)}{p_{\phi}(z|x)}\right] \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\left[\log \frac{p_{\phi}(x,z)}{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\right] + \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\left[\log \frac{q_{\theta}(z|x)}{p_{\phi}(z|x)}\right] \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\left[\log \frac{p_{\phi}(x,z)}{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\right] + \mathrm{KL}(q_{\theta}(z|x) || p_{\phi}(z|x)) \\ &\geq \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\left[\log \frac{p_{\phi}(x,z)}{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\right] \\ &= \underbrace{\mathbb E_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\left[\log p_{\phi}(x|z)\right]}_{\text{Reconstruction Term}} - \underbrace {\mathrm{KL}(q_{\theta}(z|x) || p(z))}_{\text{Latent Regularization}}\\ &= \mathcal{L}(\theta, \phi; x) \end{aligned} logpϕ​(x)​=Eqθ​(z∣x)​[logpϕ​(z∣x)pϕ​(x,z)​]=Eqθ​(z∣x)​[logqθ​(z∣x)pϕ​(x,z)​⋅pϕ​(z∣x)qθ​(z∣x)​]=Eqθ​(z∣x)​[logqθ​(z∣x)pϕ​(x,z)​]+Eqθ​(z∣x)​[logpϕ​(z∣x)qθ​(z∣x)​]=Eqθ​(z∣x)​[logqθ​(z∣x)pϕ​(x,z)​]+KL(qθ​(z∣x)∣∣pϕ​(z∣x))≥Eqθ​(z∣x)​[logqθ​(z∣x)pϕ​(x,z)​]=Reconstruction TermEqθ​(z∣x)​[logpϕ​(x∣z)]​​−Latent RegularizationKL(qθ​(z∣x)∣∣p(z))​​=L(θ,ϕ;x)​

Quick summary:

By introducing a latent variable zzz and an inaccurate posterior approximation qθ(z∣x)q_{\theta}(z|x)qθ​(z∣x), we derive a lower bound of the log-likelihood log⁡pϕ(x)\log p_{\phi}(x)logpϕ​(x), which is called Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO).

Interpretation 1.

It is the expectation of the Bayes-like ratio pϕ(x,z)qθ(z∣x)\frac{p_{\phi}(x,z)}{q_{\theta}(z|x)}qθ​(z∣x)pϕ​(x,z)​ by replacing the intractable true posterior pϕ(z∣x)p_{\phi}(z|x)pϕ​(z∣x) with the variational distribution qθ(z∣x)q_{\theta}(z|x)qθ​(z∣x).

Interpretation 2.

It consists of two terms: a reconstruction term that encourages the decoder to reconstruct xxx from zzz, and a latent regularization term that encourages the encoder distribution qθ(z∣x)q_{\theta}(z|x)qθ​(z∣x) to be close to the prior p(z)p(z)p(z).

Reason of blurriness of Standard VAE:

The standard VAE employs Gaussian distribution for both encoder and decoder.

qθ(z∣x)=N(z;μθ(x),σθ2(x)I)pϕ(x∣z)=N(x;μϕ(z),σ2I)\begin{aligned} q_{\theta}(z|x) &= \mathcal{N}(z; \mu_{\theta}(x), \sigma_{\theta}^2(x)I)\\ p_{\phi}(x|z) &= \mathcal{N}(x; \mu_{\phi}(z), \sigma^2 I) \end{aligned} qθ​(z∣x)pϕ​(x∣z)​=N(z;μθ​(x),σθ2​(x)I)=N(x;μϕ​(z),σ2I)​

The optimal decoder mean μϕ(z)\mu_{\phi}(z)μϕ​(z) is the conditional expectation E[x∣z]\mathbb{E}[x|z]E[x∣z], which is the average of all possible xxx that can be mapped to the same zzz. This averaging effect leads to blurriness in generated samples.

Derivation of optimal decoder mean:

First note that

Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡pϕ(x∣z)]=Eqθ(z∣x)[−12σ2∥x−μϕ(z)∥2+const]=−12σ2Eqθ(z∣x)[∥x−μϕ(z)∥2]+const\begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}[\log p_{\phi}(x|z)] &= \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}\left[-\frac{1}{2\sigma^2}\|x - \mu_{\phi}(z)\|^2 + \text{const}\right] \\ &= -\frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}[\|x - \mu_{\phi}(z)\|^2] + \text{const} \\ \end{aligned} Eqθ​(z∣x)​[logpϕ​(x∣z)]​=Eqθ​(z∣x)​[−2σ21​∥x−μϕ​(z)∥2+const]=−2σ21​Eqθ​(z∣x)​[∥x−μϕ​(z)∥2]+const​

And take expectation over p(x)p(x)p(x):

Ep(x)[Eqθ(z∣x)[log⁡pϕ(x∣z)]]=−12σ2Ep(x)[Eqθ(z∣x)[∥x−μϕ(z)∥2]]+const=−12σ2Eqθ(z)[Eqθ(x∣z)[∥x−μϕ(z)∥2]]+const\begin{aligned} \mathbb{E}_{p(x)}[\mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}[\log p_{\phi}(x|z)]] &= -\frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \mathbb{E}_{p(x)}[\mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z|x)}[\|x - \mu_{\phi}(z)\|^2]] + \text{const} \\ &= -\frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z)}[\mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(x|z)}[\|x - \mu_{\phi}(z)\|^2]] + \text{const} \\ \end{aligned} Ep(x)​[Eqθ​(z∣x)​[logpϕ​(x∣z)]]​=−2σ21​Ep(x)​[Eqθ​(z∣x)​[∥x−μϕ​(z)∥2]]+const=−2σ21​Eqθ​(z)​[Eqθ​(x∣z)​[∥x−μϕ​(z)∥2]]+const​

where qθ(x∣z)=p(x)qθ(z∣x)qθ(z)q_{\theta}(x|z)= \frac{p(x) q_{\theta}(z|x)}{q_{\theta}(z)}qθ​(x∣z)=qθ​(z)p(x)qθ​(z∣x)​ is the posterior distribution of xxx given zzz under the encoder.

For the inner expectation Eqθ(x∣z)[∥x−μϕ(z)∥2]\mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(x|z)}[\|x - \mu_{\phi}(z)\|^2]Eqθ​(x∣z)​[∥x−μϕ​(z)∥2], it is minimized when μϕ(z)=Eqθ(x∣z)[x]\mu_{\phi}(z) = \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(x|z)}[x]μϕ​(z)=Eqθ​(x∣z)​[x].

Think:
Which part of the structure of VAE leads to blurriness most?

The encoder or the decoder?

Answer:

It depends on your perspective.

The encoder mixes different xxx into the same zzz, create ambiguity. The Gaussian assumption of zzz enforces this mixing, otherwise the aggregate posterior qθ(z)=∫p(x)qθ(z∣x)dxq_{\theta}(z)=\int p(x) q_{\theta}(z|x) dxqθ​(z)=∫p(x)qθ​(z∣x)dx cannot match the simple prior p(z)p(z)p(z).

The Gaussian decoder reconstructs the average of these ambiguous xxx, leading to blurriness.

2.1.5 Hierarchical VAE

Model structure:

x↔decoder,pϕ(x∣z1)encoder,qθ(z1,z2∣x)z1↔decoder,pϕ(z1∣z2)encoder,qθ(z2∣z1)z2↔⋯↔decoder,pϕ(zL−1∣zL)encoder,qθ(zL∣zL−1)zLx \xleftrightarrow[\mathrm{decoder}, p_{\phi}(x|z_1)]{\mathrm{encoder}, q_{\theta}(z_1, z_2|x)} z_1 \xleftrightarrow[\mathrm{decoder}, p_{\phi}(z_1|z_2)]{\mathrm{encoder}, q_{\theta}(z_2|z_1)} z_2 \xleftrightarrow{}\cdots \xleftrightarrow[\mathrm{decoder}, p_{\phi}(z_{L-1}|z_L)]{\mathrm{encoder}, q_{\theta}(z_L|z_{L-1})}z_L xencoder,qθ​(z1​,z2​∣x)decoder,pϕ​(x∣z1​)​z1​encoder,qθ​(z2​∣z1​)decoder,pϕ​(z1​∣z2​)​z2​​⋯encoder,qθ​(zL​∣zL−1​)decoder,pϕ​(zL−1​∣zL​)​zL​

Key formulae:

Distributions

ELBO:

log⁡pϕ(x)≥Eqθ(z1:L∣x)[log⁡pϕ(x,z1:L)qθ(z1:L∣x)]=Eqθ(z1:L∣x)[log⁡pϕ(x∣z1)∏i=2Lpϕ(zi−1∣zi)p(zL)qθ(z1∣x)∏i=2Lqθ(zi∣zi−1)]=Eq[log⁡pϕ(x∣z1)]−Eq[KL(qθ(zL∣zL−1)∣∣p(zL))]−∑i=2L−1Eq[KL(qθ(zi∣zi−1)∣∣pϕ(zi∣zi+1))]−Eq[KL(qθ(z1∣x)∣∣pϕ(z1∣z2))]\begin{aligned} \log p_{\phi}(x) &\geq \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z_{1:L}|x)}\left[\log \frac{p_{\phi}(x, z_{1:L})}{q_{\theta}(z_{1:L}|x)}\right] \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{q_{\theta}(z_{1:L}|x)}\left[\log \frac{p_{\phi}(x|z_1) \prod_{i=2}^{L} p_{\phi}(z_{i-1}|z_{i}) p(z_L)}{q_{\theta}(z_1|x) \prod_{i=2}^{L} q_{\theta}(z_{i}|z_{i-1})}\right] \\ &= E_q\left[\log p_{\phi}(x|z_1)\right] - E_q\left[\mathrm{KL}(q_{\theta}(z_L|z_{L-1}) || p(z_L))\right] - \sum_{i=2}^{L-1} E_q\left[\mathrm{KL}(q_{\theta}(z_{i}|z_{i-1}) || p_{\phi}(z_{i}|z_{i+1}))\right] - E_q\left[\mathrm{KL}(q_{\theta}(z_1|x) || p_{\phi}(z_1|z_2))\right] \\ \end{aligned} logpϕ​(x)​≥Eqθ​(z1:L​∣x)​[logqθ​(z1:L​∣x)pϕ​(x,z1:L​)​]=Eqθ​(z1:L​∣x)​[logqθ​(z1​∣x)∏i=2L​qθ​(zi​∣zi−1​)pϕ​(x∣z1​)∏i=2L​pϕ​(zi−1​∣zi​)p(zL​)​]=Eq​[logpϕ​(x∣z1​)]−Eq​[KL(qθ​(zL​∣zL−1​)∣∣p(zL​))]−i=2∑L−1​Eq​[KL(qθ​(zi​∣zi−1​)∣∣pϕ​(zi​∣zi+1​))]−Eq​[KL(qθ​(z1​∣x)∣∣pϕ​(z1​∣z2​))]​
